Github Description:
This is an Unsupervised ML project
The retailer has hired us to help them create customer clusters, a.k.a "customer segments" through a data-driven approach.
They've provided us a dataset of past purchase data at the transaction level. Our task is to build a clustering model using that dataset. Our clustering model should factor in both aggregate sales patterns and specific items purchased.



-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What does that even mean?:

Imagine you go to a store with 1000s of customers - everyone wants something different - there are different clsuters formed for each different thing customers want so that personalised marketing can be sent to them about said items
    eg: nappy buying customer, gets emails of offers for nappies, while wine buying customer gets info on new wines in.
[1] 'Customer clusters/ segments' - buckets of shoppers with the same interests
[2] 'Aggregate sales patterns' - what are the overall habits of shoppers

What is Unsupervised Learning?

- type of Machine Learning
- the computer is given raw data with no labels and let it figure out the patterns itself

What K-Means is?
- type of clustering algorithm
- natural grouping of scattered dots
- k = number of clusters

Real world Impact:
1. Send targeted promotions to each grouping
2. Stock products based on what each segment buys
3. Personalise email/app content per clustering
4. Offer loyalty rewards tailored to behaviour
